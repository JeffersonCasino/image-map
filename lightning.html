Lightning Connector

The Lightning connector is a proprietary connector developed by Apple Inc. It was first introduced in 2012 with the launch of the iPhone 5, replacing the previous 30-pin dock connector used in earlier iPhone models. The Lightning connector is used for charging, syncing data, and connecting various accessories to Apple devices such as iPhones, iPads, and iPods. It is smaller in size compared to the older 30-pin connector and is reversible, meaning it can be plugged in either way, making it more convenient for users.
